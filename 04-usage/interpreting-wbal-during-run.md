# How to Use W'bal During a Run or Race

## The core principle

W'bal tells you how much high-intensity capacity you have left. Use it to make real-time pacing decisions.

## During a race

- **Start conservative:** If your W'bal drops fast in the first km, you are going out too hard.
- **Climbs:** It is normal for W'bal to drop on steep climbs if you push above CP. Plan to recover on the downhill.
- **Final kick:** Save some W'bal for the finish. A W'bal above 30–40% at the last km means you have fuel for a sprint finish.
- **W'bal at 0:** This means your anaerobic battery is empty. You cannot sustain efforts above CP. Slow down to let it partially recover before attempting another hard push.

## During interval training

- W'bal helps you know when you are ready for the next interval. Start the next rep only when W'bal has recovered to a comfortable level (e.g., 70–80% of full).
- If W'bal does not recover between reps, your rest period is too short or your CP setting is too low.

## Common mistake: ignoring the recharge

Many runners only watch W'bal go down. Remember: running easy below CP actively recharges the battery. Use easy segments strategically to recover before the next hard push.

# WbalPro Screen: All Metrics Explained

WbalPro displays multiple metrics simultaneously. Here is what each one means.

## Total Power (W)

Your current running power output in Watts. This is the main central number. Unlike pace (affected by hills) or heart rate (which lags behind effort), power responds instantly to changes in effort.

Formula concept: `Total Power = Power to run flat + Power vs wind + Power to climb`

## W'bal (Battery icon)

Your remaining anaerobic energy reserve in joules or kilojoules. The battery icon fills and drains in real time. See the "W'bal Explained" document for full detail.

## GAP (Grade Adjusted Pace)

What your pace would be on flat ground. Useful on hilly terrain to maintain consistent effort regardless of slope.

## VO2 (Average VO2max estimate)

An ongoing estimate of your VO2max based on power output per kilogram and percentage of max heart rate. Useful for tracking aerobic fitness trends over time. It is an estimate, not a lab measurement.

## PF5 (Performance Factor)

A combined score of running efficiency for the current activity. Combines power, speed, and heart rate response. Good for comparing different runs over time to track fitness progress.

## P3 / P10 / P20

Your average power over the last 3, 10, and 20 minutes respectively. Useful for pacing in races (e.g., target a specific P10 during a 10K) and for tracking power personal records at those durations.

## HR_Temp (Temperature-Adjusted Heart Rate)

Shown in some versions. Estimates what your heart rate would be at neutral temperature (15°C / 59°F), making heart-rate-based metrics like PF5 comparable across runs in different weather conditions.

**Note:** Your screen layout may vary slightly depending on the version of WbalPro installed. Labels like "Apwr" or "P180" may appear in older versions.

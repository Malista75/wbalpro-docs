# W' Prime — Your Anaerobic Battery Capacity

## What is W' Prime?

W' Prime (written as W') is the total amount of anaerobic energy available to you above your Critical Power. Think of it as the size of your battery. It is measured in kilojoules (kJ) or joules (J).

A typical W' for a trained runner might be 15–25 kJ. The larger your W', the more high-intensity work you can do before exhausting your anaerobic reserves.

W' Prime is a fixed capacity — it does not change during a run. What changes in real time is W'bal (W' Balance), which is how much of that battery you have left.

## How do I find my W'?

W' is harder to measure than CP. It requires structured testing — typically comparing your performance at multiple intensities above CP. Some coaches estimate it from race data or interval sessions.

## Where do I enter W' in WbalPro?

You enter your W' value (in kJ) in the WbalPro settings inside Garmin Connect alongside your CP. See the "Initial Setup" document.

**Also known as:** W prime, anaerobic work capacity, battery capacity, W' reserve, W prime running.
